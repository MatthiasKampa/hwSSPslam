== 6. VISION PRETRAINING DATA — replace CIFAR-100 (user directive) ============

Requirements: indoor domain, PER-PIXEL SEGMENTATION (the descriptor must be
per-cell, not per-image), RGB-D preferred, grayscale-convertible, tractable
for a 34k-param net.

  PRIMARY:  NYU Depth v2 — 1449 densely-labeled indoor RGB-D frames,
            standard 40-class mapping (chair/table/sofa/bed/desk/...),
            Kinect (same sensor class as TUM fr3 -> domain-matched eval),
            single ~2.8 GB file, standard 795/654 split. Right size, right
            domain, right labels.
  SCALE-UP: SUN RGB-D (10,335 RGB-D images, same label space, superset of
            NYU) when 1449 frames overfit.
  SYNTH BOOST (optional): Hypersim or Replica renders — perfect per-pixel
            semantics + depth, free; fine as augmentation for a descriptor
            prior.
  LONG-TERM: ScanNet v2 (request-form) — indoor RGB-D VIDEO with 2D/3D
            INSTANCE labels; also gives real object-level GT to SCORE the
            semantic map (P4b could then report P/R against real instances).
  NOT:      CIFAR (32x32 whole-image, no localization), ADE20K-general /
            Cityscapes (domain), ImageNet.

Training target: per-cell 40-class segmentation (or class-embedding) head on
GRAY(+depth) input at QVGA scale; binarize the embedding to the k-bit
descriptor; keep the BNN/XNOR budget. Evaluate: (i) seg mIoU sanity, (ii)
the P4b objmap2 harness, (iii) cross-view descriptor stability on TUM
(adjacent vs far frames) — stability is what killed census cross-view.
